# Real-World Elliott Wave Trading Examples

## Example 1: Bullish Trend
- **Description**: A bullish trend is identified where wave 1 is formed by a significant price increase, wave 2 is a correction, wave 3 is the largest wave, followed by waves 4 and 5.
- **Trade Entry**: Buy at the beginning of wave 3 anticipating the continuation.
- **Take Profit**: At the end of wave 5.
- **Stop Loss**: Just below wave 4.

### Example 2: Bearish Trend
- **Description**: In a bearish trend, wave 1 shows a decline, wave 2 is a corrective move up, followed by a strong wave 3 down.
- **Trade Entry**: Sell at the start of wave 3 expecting further downside.
- **Take Profit**: At the end of wave 5 of the downward trend.
- **Stop Loss**: Above wave 4.

## Example 3: Complex Corrections
- **Description**: Complex corrections involve multiple waves, often an ABCDE pattern.
- **Trade Entry**: Enter at the completion of wave E after seeing a reversal signal.
- **Take Profit**: At the expected wave C if bullish.
- **Stop Loss**: Below the lowest point of wave E.

# Elliott Wave Rules Reference Guide

## Core Rules
1. **Wave Structure**: The market moves in repetitive cycles of impulse and corrective waves.
2. **Five-Three Movement**: Each market cycle consists of five waves in the direction of the trend (impulse) followed by three waves against it (corrective).
3. **Rule of Alternation**: If one corrective wave is sharp, the next one will be more sideways in nature, and vice versa.
4. **Wave Overlap**: Wave 4 should not overlap with the price territory of wave 1 in any impulse wave formation.

## Fibonacci Ratio Guidelines
- **Retracement Levels**: Common retracement levels for corrective waves include 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, and 78.6%.
- **Extension Levels**: Common extension levels for impulse waves include 161.8%, 261.8%, and 423.6%.

## Corrective Patterns
1. **Zigzag**: A sharp correction consisting of three waves (A-B-C) with a 5-3-5 structure.
2. **Flat**: A sideways corrective pattern that consists of three waves (A-B-C) with a 3-3-5 structure.
3. **Triangle**: A consolidation pattern that has five waves (A-B-C-D-E) and typically indicates a continuation of the trend.

## Compliance Scoring
- Assign scores based on the adherence to Elliott Wave principles and Fibonacci levels:  
  - Perfect adherence: 10 points  
  - Minor deviations: 5 points  
  - Major deviations: 0 points

## Common Trader Errors
1. **Mislabeling Waves**: Incorrectly identifying the wave structure, leading to false signals.
2. **Ignoring Fibonacci Levels**: Failing to consider Fibonacci ratios for entry and exit points.
3. **Overtrading**: Making too many trades based on minor wave fluctuations.
4. **Confirmation Bias**: Only looking for information that supports existing views, leading to poor decision-making.
